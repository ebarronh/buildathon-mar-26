## Executive Summary

**Cleo & You** is an AI-powered corporate learning platform that transforms a company's existing knowledge — documents, PDFs, playbooks, SOPs — into personalized, multi-modal learning experiences. From realistic AI voice call simulations to podcasts, interactive Q&A, infographics, video explanations, and pitch memorization drills, Cleo meets every learner where they are and how they learn best.

The killer experience: a live AI voice simulation that feels real. Small talk. Real company context. Difficult conversations. Not always positive — because real interactions aren't. Learners practice on their terms, and optionally share scores and session notes with managers when they're ready.

Starting with sales teams as the beachhead, Cleo's engine is built to serve any corporate learning need — onboarding, product updates, customer support training, and beyond. The platform is deeply personalized per company: prompts, scenarios, tone, and content are all shaped by the organization's actual knowledge, not generic templates.

**Tagline:** Your Company's Knowledge, Your Way.

## Core Vision

### Problem Statement

Corporate learning is broken. Institutional knowledge lives inside specific people and static documents — not in systems that are always available, interactive, and adaptable to how each person learns. New hires wait weeks for overloaded trainers. Existing teams scramble before product launches with outdated decks. Managers become bottlenecks for every knowledge transfer. The result: slow ramp-up, inconsistent knowledge, and no way to practice independently.

### Problem Impact

- **New hires** lose weeks of productivity waiting for available trainers and struggling through static documentation that doesn't adapt to their learning style
- **Existing teams** lack safe, independent ways to practice and refresh before critical moments — product launches, customer calls, role changes
- **Managers** spend disproportionate time on repetitive training instead of coaching, and have no visibility into whether their team is actually ready
- **Companies** pay for a $445B global training industry that optimizes for completion rates, not behavioral change or actual competency

### Why Existing Solutions Fall Short

| Gap | What Exists Today | What's Missing |
|---|---|---|
| **Generic content** | LinkedIn Learning, Coursera — massive libraries of pre-made courses | Training built from *your* company's actual knowledge, products, and customers |
| **Single modality** | Most platforms offer video or text — pick one | Learners need variety: reading, audio, simulation, visual, interactive Q&A, practice drills |
| **No realistic practice** | Yoodli, Hyperbound offer scripted role-play | Simulations that feel *real* — small talk, real company context, difficult moments, unpredictable responses |
| **No personalization** | Platforms personalize by recommending different courses | True personalization means adapting the entire experience — format, tone, scenarios, pacing — to the learner and their company |
| **Completion theater** | LMS platforms measure "hours completed" and badge collection | No proof that training changes behavior or builds actual competency |

### Proposed Solution

Cleo & You takes a company's existing knowledge base and transforms it into a full spectrum of learning experiences:

- **AI Voice Simulations** — Live practice calls with realistic AI personas built from actual company/customer context. Small talk, objections, difficult conversations. Not scripted. Not always nice.
- **Podcast Generation** — Two-voice conversational breakdowns of complex material, listenable on the go
- **Interactive Q&A Chat** — An always-available agent that answers questions grounded in company knowledge
- **Visual Content** — Auto-generated infographics, diagrams, and explainer videos from source material
- **Reading & Tutorials** — Structured learning paths with screenshots, walkthroughs, and progressive depth
- **Pitch Memorization** — Repeat-and-refine drills where learners practice out loud until they nail it
- **Scoring & Manager Visibility** — Optional sharing of practice scores and session notes with managers, controlled entirely by the learner

The MVP focuses on **sales teams**: upload your sales playbook, product docs, and competitive intel — then practice realistic customer calls, get coached, and prove readiness.

### Key Differentiators

1. **Content transformation, not content creation** — Cleo doesn't sell courses. It transforms *your* knowledge into learning experiences. Like NotebookLM, but built for enterprise teams.
2. **Modality variety as the core value** — The same content delivered as a call simulation, a podcast, a visual explainer, a Q&A session, or a memorization drill. Every learner finds their way in.
3. **Realistic simulation that feels real** — Not scripted role-play. AI personas with real company context, personality, mood variation, and the ability to be difficult. Practice for the real thing.
4. **Deep per-company personalization** — Every deployment is shaped by the company's actual content, tone, customer profiles, and scenarios. Not templates. Fully bespoke.
5. **Learner-controlled transparency** — Learners choose what to share with managers. Trust is built from the bottom up, not surveillance from the top down.

## Target Users

### Primary Users

**1. The Learner — "Jordan, Sales Rep at a SaaS Startup"**

Jordan joined a 80-person SaaS company three weeks ago. There's a Google Drive full of product docs, a Salesforce playbook from 2024, and a "buddy" who's too busy to shadow. Jordan needs to get on real customer calls but doesn't feel ready.

With Cleo: Jordan's manager uploaded the playbook and product docs on day one. Jordan practices customer calls with an AI that sounds like a real prospect — sometimes friendly, sometimes skeptical, sometimes annoyed. After each session, Jordan sees a green/yellow/red score. When Jordan hits green on the core scenarios, they choose to share the results with their manager. Ready for real calls in days, not weeks.

- **Role:** Any employee assigned or choosing to learn — sales reps, customer support, new hires, existing team members preparing for launches
- **Motivation:** Get competent fast, practice safely, prove readiness on their own terms
- **Key need:** Multiple ways to learn the same material — calls, reading, podcasts, Q&A, drills — depending on their style and moment
- **Privacy:** Controls what scores and sessions are shared with managers. Nothing is visible until the learner decides.

**2. The Content Owner — "Priya, Sales Manager"**

Priya manages 12 reps and spends 10+ hours a week answering the same product questions and running practice sessions. Every new hire is a 3-week time sink. Every product update means re-training the whole team manually.

With Cleo: Priya uploads the new product one-pager and competitive battle cards. Cleo transforms them into practice simulations, a podcast summary, Q&A modules, and visual explainers — automatically. Priya's dashboard shows ballpark readiness across the team (green/yellow/red) as reps choose to share their results. No micromanagement. Priya coaches the yellows and reds, celebrates the greens.

- **Role:** Team lead, sales manager, department head — anyone responsible for team readiness
- **Motivation:** Stop being the bottleneck. Know the team is ready without hovering.
- **Key need:** Upload content easily, see team readiness at a glance, trust the system to do the training
- **Privacy:** Sees only what learners choose to share. Dashboard shows aggregate readiness, not surveillance.

### Secondary Users

**3. The Admin — "Alex, Head of Enablement"**

Alex oversees learning across the sales org. Sets up the company account, manages content libraries, configures which teams get which materials. May define mandatory vs. optional training paths.

- **Role:** Corporate account admin, L&D lead, enablement manager
- **Motivation:** Centralize training content, ensure consistency, report readiness to VP of Sales
- **Key need:** Easy account setup (no IT dependency), content management, org-wide readiness views

**4. The Buyer — "VP of Sales"**

Doesn't use Cleo daily. Cares about one thing: are my people ready to sell? Buys Cleo because it cuts ramp time and gives visibility into team competency — measured in readiness signals, not completion badges.

- **Role:** VP of Sales, CRO, revenue leadership
- **Motivation:** Faster ramp, higher quota attainment, proof that training spend works
- **Key need:** ROI evidence — ramp time reduction, readiness metrics, tie to revenue outcomes

### User Journey

| Stage | Learner (Jordan) | Content Owner (Priya) |
|---|---|---|
| **Setup** | Gets invited, logs in, sees content already loaded | Uploads docs — playbook, product sheets, battle cards. Takes minutes. |
| **First Experience** | Picks a learning mode — tries a practice call. It feels shockingly real. | Watches the content transform into simulations, podcasts, Q&A modules automatically. |
| **Aha Moment** | "The AI prospect just got annoyed and I had to recover. This is better than shadowing." | "I didn't have to build a single training deck. It just... worked." |
| **Core Usage** | Daily practice — calls before real meetings, podcast on commute, Q&A when stuck | Uploads new content as products evolve. Checks dashboard weekly. Coaches based on signals. |
| **Value Proof** | Shares green scores with manager. Gets on real calls with confidence. | Team ramps in days not weeks. Spends time coaching, not lecturing. VP sees the numbers. |

## Success Metrics

### User Success Metrics

| Metric | What We Measure | Target |
|---|---|---|
| **Time to Green** | How quickly a learner reaches "green" (ready) on a given topic | Average 2 weeks per topic, down from 4-6 weeks of traditional onboarding |
| **Learner Confidence** | Learners feel prepared before real interactions — calls, meetings, conferences | Qualitative: learners voluntarily share green scores with managers |
| **Knowledge Accessibility** | Time to get an answer to a product question | Instant (via Q&A chat) vs. current "wait for someone available" |
| **Practice Frequency** | Learners proactively using simulations before real-world moments | Learners practice before key meetings, product launches, and conference attendance |
| **Modality Adoption** | Learners using multiple learning formats, not just one | At least 2 modalities used per learner (e.g., simulation + podcast, or Q&A + reading) |

|---|---|
| **Paying customers** | 4-6 companies |
| **Go-to-market** | Direct sales with optional free trial |

### Key Performance Indicators

**Leading Indicators (predict success):**
- Number of simulations run per user per week
- Content upload frequency by managers (shows ongoing engagement, not one-time setup)
- Percentage of learners reaching green within 2-week target
- Number of learners voluntarily sharing scores with managers

**Lagging Indicators (prove success):**
- Customer-reported ramp time reduction (before/after Cleo)
- Reduction in internal training hours logged by managers and technical teams
- Deal close rates for reps who practiced on Cleo vs. those who didn't
- Net revenue retention — customers expand seats over time
- Customer count: 4-6 paying companies in year 1

**Anti-Metrics (what we refuse to optimize for):**
- Completion rates — finishing modules means nothing if behavior doesn't change
- Time on platform — more time could mean the product is confusing, not valuable
- Badge/certificate count — vanity metrics that satisfy HR dashboards, not learners

## MVP Scope

### Core Features

**The MVP is one thing done exceptionally well: realistic AI voice simulations built from your company's knowledge.**

| Feature | Description | MVP Scope |
|---|---|---|
| **Content Ingestion** | Upload company knowledge that powers everything | Text and images. Drag-and-drop simple. No IT required. |
| **AI Voice Simulation** | Live practice calls with realistic AI personas | Real small talk, real company context, mood variation, difficult conversations. Not scripted. |
| **Scoring & Readiness** | Green/yellow/red assessment after each session | AI-assessed performance. Learner sees their score immediately. |
| **Optional Sharing** | Learner chooses to share results with manager | Simple share action — learner controls what and when. Privacy-first. |
| **User Accounts** | Basic auth, company workspace, invite team members | Company signs up, invites learners, uploads content. Done. |

**What makes this MVP enough to land customers:**
- A sales manager uploads their playbook and product docs
- Within minutes, their team can practice realistic customer calls
- The AI prospect knows the company's products, uses real objections, and doesn't always play nice
- Reps practice until they hit green, then share results when ready
- Manager knows who's ready without running a single training session

### Out of Scope for MVP

| Feature | Why Not Yet | When |
|---|---|---|
| **Manager Dashboard** | Learners can share scores directly. Full dashboard is a fast follow. | v1.1 — shortly after launch |
| **Podcast Generation** | High value, but not the core "aha" moment | v2 |
| **Interactive Q&A Chat** | Useful, but simulations prove the concept faster | v2 |
| **Video/Infographics** | Content transformation modalities come after the engine is proven | v2-v3 |
| **Reading/Tutorials** | Structured learning paths build on top of proven content ingestion | v2 |
| **Pitch Memorization Drills** | Repeat-and-refine drills are a natural extension of voice | v2 |
| **PDF Ingestion** | Start with text + images, expand format support quickly | v1.1 |
| **VR Experiences** | May come back when the platform and economics justify it | v3+ |
| **Advanced Analytics** | Deep performance trends, team comparisons, ROI reporting | v2 |

### MVP Success Criteria

| Gate | Signal | Threshold |
|---|---|---|
| **Problem Validation** | Sales managers say "this is what I needed" after seeing their team practice | 3+ companies in pilot actively using it |
| **User Adoption** | Learners voluntarily return to practice without being forced | 50%+ of invited learners complete 3+ sessions in first 2 weeks |
| **Value Proof** | At least one customer reports faster ramp or reduced training burden | Qualitative confirmation from 2+ pilot customers |
| **Simulation Quality** | Learners describe simulations as "realistic" or "better than role-play with a colleague" | 70%+ positive feedback on realism |
